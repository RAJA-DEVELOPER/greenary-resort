Ancient Waters

The River Has
Always Known the Way

Long before trails were mapped, water found its own path through Terravana's ancient rock — carving gorges, creating plunge pools, sculpting moss-covered amphitheatres of sound and mist. These seven waterfalls are among the most beautiful secrets the forest holds.

Our waterfall guides combine geological knowledge, ecological storytelling, and a deep reverence for these sacred sites — many of which hold ceremonial significance for the local indigenous community.

Meet the Falls

Fall I · The Crown Jewel

Devakund — The Sacred Veil

At 60 metres, Devakund is Terravana's most spectacular waterfall. The water falls in a single unbroken veil into a 15-metre deep emerald plunge pool. Considered sacred by the local Kanni tribe, the site is accessible only with a certified guide.

Capture the Flow

Waterfall Photography

Long Exposure

To capture that silky water effect, bring an ND filter (ND8 or ND64) and a sturdy tripod. Ideal shutter speed for our falls is between 0.5 to 2 seconds.

Gear Protection

The mist off Devakund and Twin Veils is intense. A rain sleeve for your camera and microfibre cloths for constant lens wiping are essential.

Best Light

Overcast days provide the best even lighting. On sunny days, the golden hour (6:00 AM - 7:30 AM) prevents harsh contrast between the water and dark rock.

Be Prepared

What to Bring

Water Shoes or Amphibious Boots

Wet basalt rock is extremely slippery. Shoes with high-traction Vibram soles are highly recommended.

Dry Bag

Essential for keeping your phone, camera, and dry layers safe during river crossings and near the mist zones.

Quick-Dry Layers

Avoid cotton. Bring synthetic or merino wool layers that retain warmth even when damp.

Ancient Stories

Myth of the Seven Falls

According to the oral traditions of the Kanni community, the seven waterfalls were not carved by rivers, but were tears wept by the Mountain Spirit when the first trees were cut. Today, these sites remain deeply sacred. We ask all guests to respect these areas—keeping voices low and leaving nothing behind.
