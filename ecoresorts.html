How We Stay Low Impact

The systems behind the serenity.

The calm you feel in the rooms comes from many quiet decisions: fewer rooms, low-rise structures, shaded paths, native planting, filtered water, and a kitchen that plans around what the region can responsibly provide.

Water
Rainwater harvesting, low-flow fittings, greywater treatment, and refill stations.
Energy
Solar hot water, warm low-energy lighting, and passive cooling before mechanical cooling.
Waste
Composting, bulk purchasing, refill amenities, and no single-use plastic bottles.
Habitat
Native planting, quiet zones, controlled lighting, and wildlife corridor monitoring.
Forest Kitchen

Food that belongs to the season.

Meals at Terravana are generous without being heavy. Breakfasts are built for the trail, lunches are fresh and cooling, and dinners lean into smoke, spice, grain, herbs, and slow conversations.

  • Trail breakfast with fruit, millet, eggs or plant-based options, and fresh bread.
  • Packed lunches for waterfall walks, safaris, and long ridge routes.
  • Evening menus with smoked vegetables, local curries, herb teas, and fire breads.
  • Dietary requests handled during booking with advance notice.

Are the lodges air-conditioned

Selected rooms include cooling, but most comfort comes from shade, ventilation, high roofs, and evening temperature drops.

Is the resort good for families

Yes. Ridge Villas are best for families, and the guide team can plan shorter walks for children.

Can I work remotely

Common lounges have Wi-Fi and charging points. Rooms are kept quieter and more restorative.

What should I pack

Comfortable walking shoes, neutral layers, rain protection in monsoon, a refill bottle, and a small daypack.

Are pets allowed

Pets are not allowed in forest stay zones because of wildlife safety and trail sensitivity.

Can I request a private dinner

Yes. Deck dinners, private campfires, and celebration menus can be arranged in advance.
