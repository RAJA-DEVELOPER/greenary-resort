A Living Wilderness

Where Every Shadow Has a Name

Terravana Wilds sits within one of the most biodiverse ecosystems in the region - a mosaic of tropical rainforest, cloud forest, and wetland habitats supporting over 320 documented species.

Our naturalist team has spent years mapping wildlife corridors, identifying individual animals, and building the deepest possible knowledge of this living system - so that when you visit, you step into a world already known and loved by your guide.

Meet the Residents

Icon Species of Terravana

• Vulnerable
Forest Leopard
Panthera pardus

Our most elusive resident. Best spotted at dusk in the eastern corridors. Approximately 6-8 individuals inhabit Terravana's core zone.

• Endangered
Asian Elephant
Elephas maximus

A family group of 14-18 individuals moves through Terravana seasonally. Dawn encounters at the salt lick are among our most treasured experiences.

• Vulnerable
Great Hornbill
Buceros bicornis

The forest's most spectacular aviator. Nest monitoring of 3 pairs is part of our active conservation program. Unmistakable in flight and call.

• Vulnerable
Sun Bear
Helarctos malayanus

The world's smallest bear, shy and rarely seen. Camera trap evidence suggests a thriving local population of 8-12 individuals in our western zone.

Ecosystems

Five Habitats,
One Forest

Terravana's unique geography creates a stacking of distinct ecological zones - each home to its own community of species.

Field Notes
How We Track Movement

Our guides read fresh prints, alarm calls, fruiting trees, and camera trap signals before each safari. Routes shift daily so guests follow real wildlife activity instead of fixed tourist circuits.

Lowland Rainforest · 200-600m
Primary forest floor with emergent giants, fig trees, and the highest mammal density. Home to elephants, sambar deer, and porcupines.
Riparian Corridor · Riverbanks
Linear forest along river channels - critical wildlife superhighways. Otters, kingfishers, and elephant drinking sites.
Cloud Forest · 1,200-2,200m
Perpetually mist-draped and carpeted with moss. Home to the leopard, hornbill colonies, and endemic orchid species.
Alpine Shrubland · 2,200m+
Windswept and spare. Rare raptors soar here - crested serpent eagles, mountain hawk-eagles, and during migration, steppe eagles.
Grassland Clearings
Open glades maintained by elephant browsing - prime locations for spotting gaur, spotted deer, and resident leopards at dusk.

Rhythms of the Wild

Seasonal Migrations

The Great Butterfly Migration

Millions of Blue Tigers and Crows migrate through our valleys just before the northeast monsoon. A breathtaking natural phenomenon.

Peak: October - November
Raptor Flyway

Terravana sits on a major flyway for migratory birds of prey heading south for the winter. Steppe eagles and harriers are common sights above the canopy.

Peak: November - January
Elephant Corridors

During the dry season, elephant herds move from the higher elevations down to our riverine forests in search of water and fresh bamboo.

Peak: March - May
Our Ethics

Wildlife Code of Conduct

  • 1.

    Zero Interference

    We never bait, feed, or call out to wildlife. We observe them in their natural state.

  • 2.

    Maintain Distance

    We strictly adhere to minimum approach distances for all species to prevent stress and habituation.

  • 3.

    Silence is Golden

    Whispers only on the trail. Mobile phones must be switched to silent mode before entering the forest.
