Built Around The Wild

Terravana Wilds exists to make travel feel intimate, ecological, and deeply rooted in place.

We did not build a resort in the forest. We built a quieter way to belong to it.

Who We Are

A forest-first eco retreat shaped by people who know this land.

Terravana Wilds began as a small conservation camp on the edge of an old rainforest corridor. Today it is an immersive eco-adventure resort where lodges, trails, kitchens, and guide teams are designed around the rhythms of the land.

Every stay supports habitat care, local employment, research partnerships, and slow travel experiences that leave the forest stronger than we found it.

  • Small group adventures led by trained naturalists and local trackers.
  • Low-impact lodges built with passive cooling, rainwater harvesting, and refill systems.
  • Seasonal menus built around nearby farms, forest herbs, and traditional cooking.
  • Revenue shared with community education and conservation programmes.

Ecology First

Routes, lodges, and guest numbers are planned around wildlife movement, water sensitivity, and seasonal restoration windows.

Local Partnership

Guides, cooks, craftspeople, drivers, and storytellers from nearby villages shape the Terravana experience every day.

Real Wilderness

No staged wildlife. No scripted forest. Some mornings are dramatic, some are silent, and both are part of the truth.

Our Journey

From field camp to conscious wilderness hospitality.

2015

A small guide team begins mapping ethical trekking routes with local forest communities.

2018

The first low-impact cottages open with solar heating, refill stations, and local food programmes.

2021

Terravana launches its wildlife corridor fund and supports long-term camera trap monitoring.

2025

The resort becomes carbon neutral and expands guest learning through workshops, research walks, and youth camps.

Living Design

Architecture that listens before it speaks.

Our spaces are designed around shade, wind, water, and silence. Rooflines follow canopy movement, paths curve around root systems, and every viewing deck is placed where guests can pause without disturbing the habitat.

Passive Cool

Cross-ventilation, shaded walls, and natural materials reduce mechanical cooling needs.

Water Wise

Rain capture, greywater reuse, and low-flow systems protect dry-season supply.

Rooms tucked into green.

Luxury here means waking to leaves, walking barefoot on cool stone, and feeling the day arrive slowly.

What We Believe

Four principles guide every guest experience.

Regenerate

Every trail, menu, and lodge choice should support the land that hosts it.

Respect

Guests, guides, wildlife, and communities deserve care without performance.

Reveal

We help guests notice the quiet details: tracks, calls, light, water, and time.

Restore

Adventure should leave people energized, not extracted from themselves.

Conservation Lab

The forest teaches, we document.

Behind the guest experience is a quieter layer of research, maintenance, and protection work that keeps Terravana honest.

Camera Trap Monitoring

Seasonal wildlife movement is documented with non-invasive field cameras and guide observations.

Water Watch

Streams and waterfall-fed pools are checked through the year to guide route closures and guest limits.

Habitat Days

Guests can join planting, nursery care, seed collection, and trail restoration on selected mornings.

Our Promise

Luxury can be gentle.

For us, luxury is not excess. It is clean air, careful food, guides who remember your name, rooms that sleep cool without waste, and mornings where the forest decides the pace.

When you leave Terravana, we want you rested, amazed, and more protective of wild places than when you arrived.
